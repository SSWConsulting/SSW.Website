G'day Developers!

This month I have a video for you which shows the recent work completed by Gerard Beckerleg and the guys, who shipped the National Australia Day website. They introduced the client to prioritising and adding extra functionality to their existing website by utilising Scrum methodology.

The team worked closely together under the guidance and planning of Gerard as Scrum Master keeping Bucky Toller highly involved in the project as Product Owner.

I was impressed that the communication was open and constant during the process, keeping the client abreast of the project and encouraging feedback. As you know I am a fan of Scrum and I think the video shows the benefits.

I am very proud of Gerard and the boys and their implementation of Scrum. It is great to hear such positive feedback from the client.

Tell me the good and bad of how Scrum is working for you.

Adam Cogan, SSW

Session 1: Looking at Sitefinity 5.1 from a developers view

CMS’s are notoriously nice for content editors (who are just happy with adding pages and editing them in a HTML or WYSIWYG control), but nasty for developers trying to add functionality.

Drew Robson will give his take on how Sitefinity 5.1 works from a developer’s point of view, and how the new MVC support alleviates the above problem.

Session 2: Tips and Tricks with LINQ Expressions

Although LINQ Expressions have been around for years, as a developer are you using their most powerful features? Drew will demonstrate how you can transform your application data pipeline with LINQ Expressions and LINQ to Entities, resulting in cleaner code and greater reusability. You would be amazed at the amount of code you save when you see the refactoring take place.

The new VS 2012 will be used, but can be targeted to .NET 3.5, 4.0 or 4.5.
