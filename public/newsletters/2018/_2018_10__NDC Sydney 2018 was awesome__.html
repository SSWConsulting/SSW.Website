NDC Sydney 2018 was awesome

September has come and gone and NDC Sydney is over. SSW was a large part of this great conference, which is personally my favourite event of the year.

Of course, I love going to NDC Oslo, but there’s nothing like having the Norwegians come to your home town. This was the 3rd annual Sydney event and it was awesome. The conference is a 5-day event (September 18-21), composed of 2 days of workshops and 3 days of presentations. SSW and lots of companies really work hard for this event – we made sure our guys were presenting leading-edge technologies and practices and we also had a cool booth and cool marketing collateral, such as t-shirts and brochures, a live Azure conversation bot, a HoloLens and more.

This year, a bunch of SSW developers worked really hard to have a conversation bot working against live data that has real business benefits. It used Azure, Google, Dynamics CRM, Office 365, SharePoint, and answered some really cool questions. Our team worked really hard and finished it the night before the event!

SSW was an event partner, so we had a booth showing off our Sophie-bot and our Sophie-receptionist, and everyone looked great in our 3 new SSW t-shirts.

Blazor: Run C# in the browser - presented by Brendan Richards

Blazor is a new experimental framework from Microsoft that uses web assembly to run a .NET framework inside the browser. This talk covers getting started with blazor, before demonstrating its capabilities and readiness for typical line of business applications by building a UI over the venerable Northwind database.



About the presenter

Brendan is a solution architect at SSW who spends most of his time “at the coal face” delivering web solutions for his clients. When not developing, Brendan also teaches .NET Core and Angular at SSW FireBootCamp courses. Brendan’s current list of favourite things include: .NET Core, Linux, Azure, Elastic Search, Angular and ngrx.
