The Business Case for the SharePoint Social Features

Marlon Marescia the Sales Manager at SSW has just interviewed Rob Finney from Microsoft on his podcast https://www.talkingOffice365.com/ where they discussed how these social features of SharePoint can make a business more productive.

SharePoint includes many social features which not only improve communication between staff members, but gives them the ability to improve the find-ability of important data as they work. This makes data access easier every time SharePoint is used.

During the interview Marlon and Rob discussed the following:

  • An overview of the social features in SharePoint including:
    • My Sites
    • Profile pages
    • Blogs
    • Wikis
    • Tagging
    • People search
    • Skillsets
    • Org charts
  • The business challenges solved by these social features of SharePoint
  • Examples of how businesses have used the social features successfully
  • The types of businesses that get maximum value from these features

Project Cloudbreaker: Sync your CRM

Speaker: George Doubinski

Welcome to Just Cloud Inc - a company that runs just about everything in the cloud. They have been using Microsoft CRM Online 2011 since January 2011. The sales, marketing & customer service departments love it. They also moved their accounting system from the desktop to the cloud and as of July 2011 (new financial year), use only cloud-based accounting. The legal & accounting departments are loving it. Now they face the problem of multiple data entry, duplicate information and discrepancies between sales and accounting. The CEO & CIO are not happy. Enter project "Cloudbreaker": integration of Microsoft CRM Online 2011 and other cloud-based systems such as accounting and email marketing using Windows Azure-based services and Microsoft Sync Framework.

Continuous Delivery with TFS, msbuild and msdeploy

Speaker: Peter Gfader

If you are deploying your software manually, you are doing it wrong. If you are deploying once a month, you are doing it wrong. If you as a developer are deploying from Visual Studio by clicking "Publish", you are doing it wrong. If a bug-fix takes you 1 hour but your customer needs to wait a week until he gets it, you are doing it wrong. Manual deployments are NOT fun. See a good way on how to automate the deployment with TFS 2010, msbuild and msdeploy.
