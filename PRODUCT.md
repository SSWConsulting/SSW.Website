# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Enterprise tech decision-makers** evaluating SSW for software consulting, AI strategy, or Azure engagements. They arrive skeptical, assess credibility fast, and need to reach a real person without friction.
- **Developers and technologists** considering employment or community involvement with SSW. They respect technical depth and notice when a company practices what it preaches.
- **Community members and event attendees** following SSW's user groups, training courses, and live streams. They see SSW as a hub for the local and regional tech community.

## Product Purpose

SSW Consulting's primary public presence: a marketing and credibility hub for a software consulting firm specialising in enterprise .NET, AI, Azure, and software architecture. The site exists to convert enterprise visitors into clients, attract technical talent, and reinforce SSW's standing as a genuine industry contributor — not just a vendor. Success means a visitor can immediately gauge SSW's depth, find a relevant service or person, and initiate contact without obstacle.

## Brand Personality

Expert and direct. Authority earned through substance: decades of real projects, public technical content, community events, and named people. No marketing fluff, no vague claims. The site speaks like a senior consultant, not a brochure.

Three words: **authoritative, direct, connected.**

## Anti-references

- Generic IT outsourcer aesthetics: stock photo teams, vague capability statements, "we deliver value" copy.
- Offshore consulting pages: price-first, faceless, zero community signal.
- Startup hype: AI buzzword saturation, gradient-heavy hero sections, big claims with no evidence.
- Fluffy corporate: sanitised language, low information density, buried contact paths.
- Unapproachable institutions: no faces, no names, contact forms as the only route.

## Design Principles

1. **Depth over decoration.** Every surface earns its place through substance. Information density is a feature, not a problem to be softened with whitespace.
2. **Humans first.** Real people, named faces, accessible contact. SSW's authority comes through its consultants, community figures, and events — not an abstract brand.
3. **Practice what you preach.** SSW sells technical excellence in AI and Azure. The site itself must feel technically sophisticated and well-crafted. Sloppiness is self-defeating.
4. **Show, don't tell.** Authority through track record: case studies, events, published content, community contributions. Claims without evidence are indistinguishable from competitors.
5. **Community is the product.** SSW's industry involvement — user groups, training, live streams, open source — is not a footnote. It belongs at the centre of the narrative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Ensure adequate contrast ratios for SSW Red (`#cc4141`) on white and dark backgrounds. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable.
