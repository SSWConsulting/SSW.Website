  ## Why Upgrade Your Legacy .NET Applications?


  There are several common reasons why organizations choose to upgrade:


  * Unsupported Versions: If your current .NET version is no longer supported,
  you’re exposed to security risks and miss out on critical bug fixes and
  performance improvements.

  * Operating System Compatibility: New .NET versions often support the latest
  operating systems. This enables you to leverage new features and enhanced
  security updates provided by the OS.

  * New APIs & Performance Enhancements: Upgrades bring important API changes,
  performance optimizations, and advanced security measures. This means faster,
  more secure, and scalable applications.

  ## Personalising Your Data Strategy: RAG vs. Post‑Training


  When upgrading your applications, it’s crucial to think about how your AI or
  data processing components will work with personalised data. There are two
  effective approaches:


  * Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG):
    Ideal for data that changes frequently—such as seasonal information or dynamic business updates. With RAG, your system can pull in the latest data during each query, ensuring that responses remain current and relevant.
  * Post‑Training:
    This approach involves fine‑tuning your model on a fixed dataset, creating a specialised solution tailored to your business needs. It works best for relatively static data that defines your application’s context.

  Choosing between RAG and post‑training depends on your use case. For more on
  AI development best practices, see SSW’s [Rules to Better AI
  Development](https://www.ssw.com.au/rules/rules-to-better-ai-development/).
